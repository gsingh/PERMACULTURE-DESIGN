### Passive Solar Design Features (CRITICAL for Kurukshetra Heat)

**South-Facing (Front):**
- **Large windows:** 60-70% of south wall glazed (winter sun, summer light)
- **Deep overhang:** 2-3 ft projection (blocks summer sun 35-40Â° angle, allows winter sun 15-20Â° angle)
- **Covered porch:** 6-10 ft depth (outdoor living space, shaded in summer)
- **Thermal mass:** Concrete floor OR exposed brick walls (stores cool at night, releases during day)

**North-Facing (Back):**
- **Small windows:** 20-30% of north wall glazed (minimize heat gain)
- **Thick walls:** 9-12 inch walls (insulation from cold winter winds)
- **Buffer spaces:** Kitchen/bathroom on north (less critical for thermal comfort)

**East-West Walls:**
- **Minimal glazing:** No large windows on east/west (morning/evening sun too intense)
- **Shading:** Trees planted 10-15 ft from east/west walls (Year 2-3 shading)

**Roof:**
- **Light-colored metal:** Reflects 50-70% solar radiation
- **Minimum 30Â° slope:** Rain runoff, monsoon readiness
- **Insulation:** Rockwool or straw bale under roof (reduces heat by 8-10Â°C)
- **Ridge vent:** Allow hot air to escape at peak

**Ventilation:**
- **Cross-ventilation:** North-south window alignment (capture prevailing winds)
- **High windows:** Clerestory windows on north wall (hot air exhaust)
- **Ceiling fans:** 3-4 fans per house (electricity consumption: 75W vs 1,500W AC)

### Housing Construction Timeline

**Month 1-2: Site Preparation & Foundation (March-April)**

**Weeks 1-2: Site Staking & Preparation**
- Task 1: Mark house corners with stakes (surveyor or DIY with measuring tape + A-frame)
- Task 2: Excavate foundation trenches (2-3 ft deep, 1.5 ft wide)
- Task 3: Level foundation base, compact soil
- Task 4: Install foundation formwork (wooden or metal)
- **Milestone:** Formwork inspection before concrete pour

**Weeks 3-4: Foundation Pour & Curing**
- Task 5: RCC foundation concrete pour (M15 grade minimum)
- Task 6: Plinth beam construction (connects foundation, supports walls)
- Task 7: Curing (minimum 7 days, water 2-3x daily)
- Task 8: Backfill foundation, compact soil
- **Milestone:** Foundation strength test (after 28 days for full strength)

**Month 3-4: Wall Construction (May-June)**

**Conventional Brick:**
- Week 1-2: First course to lintel level (6-7 ft)
- Week 3-4: Lintel installation, walls to roof level (10-12 ft)
- Week 5-6: Curing, plastering prep

**CEB Blocks:**
- Week 1-2: CEB block production (1,200-1,500 blocks per house)
- Week 3-4: First course to lintel level
- Week 5-6: Lintel installation, walls to roof level
- Week 7-8: Minimal plastering (exterior only if desired)

**Milestone:** Wall completion inspection
