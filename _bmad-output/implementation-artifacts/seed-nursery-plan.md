## ðŸŒ± PROPAGATION METHODS

### Seed Starting (Vegetables, Annuals)

**Process:**
1. **Seed Selection:**
   - Open-pollinated varieties (can save seeds)
   - Heirloom varieties (flavor, local adaptation)
   - Source: Saved seeds, Seed banks, reputable suppliers (Organic India, Seeds of Life)

2. **Seed Starting Mix:**
   - **Recipe:** 40% cocopeat, 30% vermicompost, 30% perlite/vermiculite
   - **Purpose:** Light, sterile, good drainage (prevents damping-off)
   - **Cost:** â‚¹300-500 for 50 liters (enough for 100+ trays)

3. **Containers:**
   - **72-cell seed trays:** Plastic, reusable, uniform cells (â‚¹50-100 each)
   - **Biodegradable pots:** Newspaper pots, coconut coir pots (transplant directly)
   - **Recycled containers:** Yogurt cups, egg cartons (free, drill drainage holes)

4. **Sowing:**
   - Fill cells, firm gently (no air pockets)
   - Sow seeds (depth = 2-3Ã— seed size, follow packet instructions)
   - Label (variety, date) - crucial for tracking
   - Water gently (mist spray, avoid washing seeds away)

5. **Germination:**
   - **Temperature:** 20-25Â°C ideal (most vegetables)
   - **Moisture:** Keep consistently moist (not waterlogged)
   - **Light:** Some seeds need light (lettuce), others dark (tomatoes) - check requirements
   - **Duration:** 3-14 days (varies by species)

6. **Transplanting to Larger Pots:**
   - When 2-4 true leaves appear (not cotyledons)
   - Move to 3-4 inch pots with richer soil mix (50% soil, 40% compost, 10% cocopeat)
   - Grow 2-4 weeks before field transplanting

### Tree Propagation (Fruit Trees, Timber)

**Method 1: Seed (Slow, Variable Genetics)**
- **Use For:** Rootstock, timber trees (eucalyptus, neem), experimental
- **Process:**
  - Collect fresh seeds (ripe fruit, viable seeds)
  - Stratify if needed (cold treatment for temperate species)
  - Sow in pots or beds (2-4 inch deep)
  - Germination: 2-8 weeks (varies widely)
- **Timeline:** 1-2 years to transplant size (2-3 ft)

**Method 2: Cuttings (Fast, Clones Parent Plant)**
- **Use For:** Pomegranate, fig, mulberry, guava (easy rooters)
- **Process:**
  - Take 6-12 inch semi-hardwood cuttings (pencil thickness)
  - Remove lower leaves, keep 2-3 leaves at top
  - Dip in rooting hormone (optional, â‚¹200-400 per bottle)
  - Stick in rooting medium (50% cocopeat, 50% perlite)
  - Keep humid (mist daily or cover with plastic dome)
  - Roots in 3-8 weeks
- **Timeline:** 3-6 months to transplant size

**Method 3: Grafting (Best Genetics, Faster Fruiting)**
- **Use For:** Mango, citrus, apple, guava (improve fruit quality)
- **Process:**
  - Grow rootstock from seed (1 year to pencil thickness)
  - Source scion wood (from known productive tree)
  - Graft in spring (Feb-March, active growth)
  - Techniques: Cleft graft, whip-and-tongue, budding
  - Success rate: 60-80% with practice
- **Timeline:** 1 year to transplant size (after grafting)
- **Skill:** Requires training (watch YouTube, practice sessions with expert)

**Method 4: Air Layering (Difficult Species)**
- **Use For:** Litchi, mango, guava (mature plants faster)
- **Process:**
  - Select branch (pencil to thumb thickness)
  - Wound bark (remove 1-inch ring)
  - Apply rooting hormone
  - Wrap with moist sphagnum moss + plastic (seal ends)
  - Roots form in 2-4 months
  - Cut below roots, pot up
- **Timeline:** 6-12 months to transplant size

### Native Plant Propagation (Zone 5 Restoration)

**Division (Perennials, Grasses):**
- Dig mature clumps, divide into smaller sections (3-5 shoots each)
- Pot up or plant directly in nursery beds
- Water consistently until established

## ðŸŒ¾ SEED SAVING PROGRAM

### Why Save Seeds?

**Benefits:**
- **Adaptation:** Plants adapt to local climate, soil, pests over generations
- **Cost Savings:** Eliminate seed purchase costs (â‚¹5,000-10,000/year saved)
- **Genetic Diversity:** Preserve heirloom varieties, rare local types
- **Resilience:** Insurance against commercial seed unavailability
- **Cultural Heritage:** Maintain traditional varieties, share with community

**Challenges:**
- **Cross-Pollination:** Requires isolation or hand-pollination (cucurbits, brassicas)
- **Hybrid Varieties:** F1 hybrids don't breed true (must use open-pollinated only)
- **Storage:** Requires cool, dry, dark conditions (moisture and heat destroy viability)

---

### Seed Saving Process (Tomatoes Example)

**1. Selection:**
- Choose best plants (vigorous, disease-free, productive)
- Select best fruits (size, flavor, shape)
- Label plants early (mark with ribbon, observe all season)

**2. Harvesting:**
- Wait until fruit fully ripe (even overripe)
- Scoop seeds into container

**3. Fermentation (Tomatoes, Cucumbers):**
- Add water to seeds, let sit 2-4 days (room temperature)
- Fermentation removes germination inhibitors, kills pathogens
- Viable seeds sink, pulp/bad seeds float

**4. Cleaning:**
- Pour off pulp and floating seeds
- Rinse remaining seeds in sieve (running water)
- Spread on paper towel or screen

**5. Drying:**
- Dry in shade (sun can damage seeds)
- Stir occasionally (prevent clumping)
- Fully dry = snap when bent (7-14 days)

**6. Storage:**
- Paper envelopes or cloth bags (breathable)
- Label: Variety, date, notes (flavor, vigor)
- Store in airtight container with silica gel packets (moisture control)
- Location: Cool (10-15Â°C), dark (closet, refrigerator)

**7. Viability Testing (Next Season):**
- Germination test: Sow 10 seeds, count how many sprout
- >80% germination: Excellent
- 50-80%: Acceptable, sow thicker
- <50%: Poor, source new seeds

## ðŸ› COMMON PROBLEMS & SOLUTIONS

### Damping-Off (Seedling Disease)

**Symptoms:** Seedlings collapse at soil line, stems blackened

**Causes:** Fungal infection (Pythium, Fusarium), overwatering, poor air circulation

**Prevention:**
- Use sterile seed starting mix (avoid garden soil in seed trays)
- Don't overwater (moist, not soggy)
- Improve air circulation (fans, proper spacing)
- Bottom watering (reduces surface moisture)

**Treatment:** Remove affected seedlings, reduce watering, apply cinnamon powder (natural fungicide)

---

### Leggy Seedlings (Etiolation)

**Symptoms:** Tall, weak, pale stems, small leaves

**Causes:** Insufficient light, high temperature, crowding

**Prevention:**
- Provide bright light (6-8 hours daily, supplement with grow lights if needed)
- Cool temperatures (18-22Â°C for most vegetables)
- Adequate spacing (air circulation)

**Treatment:** Transplant deeper (bury leggy stem up to first leaves), increase light

---

### Poor Cutting Success (No Roots)

**Symptoms:** Cuttings wilt, turn brown, die before rooting

**Causes:** Wrong timing, too dry, too wet, wrong wood hardness

**Solutions:**
- Take cuttings in optimal season (spring for most species)
- Use semi-hardwood (not too soft, not too woody)
- Maintain high humidity (mist daily or plastic tent)
- Use rooting hormone (increases success rate 20-40%)

---

### Low Germination Rate

**Symptoms:** Few seeds sprout, uneven germination

**Causes:** Old seeds, wrong temperature, improper depth, pre-treatment needed

**Solutions:**
- Test seed viability before large sowing (sample germination test)
- Check temperature requirements (heat mat for warm-season crops)
- Correct sowing depth (general rule: 2-3Ã— seed diameter)
- Scarify/stratify seeds if needed (hard coats, cold stratification for temperate species)
