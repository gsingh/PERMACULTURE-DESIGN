### Monsoon Timing is CRITICAL!

**Why Plant in Monsoon?**
- ‚úÖ Natural rainfall (no irrigation cost)
- ‚úÖ Trees establish deep roots (chase water table down)
- ‚úÖ Cooler temperatures (less transplant shock)
- ‚úÖ High humidity (reduced water stress)
- ‚úÖ Soil moisture (easy digging, good root contact)

**Kurukshetra Monsoon Pattern:**

| Month | Rainfall | Planting Status | Success Rate |
|-------|----------|----------------|--------------|
| **June** | 50-100mm (early monsoon) | ‚ö†Ô∏è Can start, but risky | 60-70% |

| **August** | 100-200mm (good) | ‚úÖ **OPTIMAL WINDOW** | 85-90% |
| **September** | 50-100mm (declining) | ‚ö†Ô∏è Last chance | 70-80% |
| **October** | 10-30mm (monsoon end) | ‚ùå TOO LATE (dry season approaching) | 40-50% |

**Planting Steps (July-August, during monsoon):**

**STEP 1: Prepare Tree**
- Remove sapling from pot/bag carefully (don't break root ball)
- If bare-root, soak roots in water 2-4 hours before planting
- Inspect roots - cut any broken/diseased roots with sharp secateurs
- Keep root ball moist (cover with wet burlap until ready to plant)

**STEP 2: Adjust Pit**
- Remove covering from pre-dug pit (board/plastic)
- Check depth: Place tree in pit, root collar should be 2-3 inches ABOVE ground level
  - Why? Tree will settle, and root collar below ground = rot risk
- If too deep: Add soil to raise level
- If too shallow: Remove some soil

**STEP 3: Planting**
- Place tree in CENTER of pit, straight vertical (use spirit level if available)
- Backfill with pit soil mix, gently firm around roots (no air pockets!)
- Build soil up to root collar (NOT above - leave exposed)
- Create water basin: Form 4-6 inch high ring of soil around tree (3 ft diameter)
  - This basin will catch rainwater and direct to roots

**STEP 4: Initial Watering**
- Fill basin with water (10-15 liters) - even in monsoon! 
- Why? Settles soil, ensures good root contact
- Let water drain completely
- Add more soil if settling revealed roots

**STEP 5: Mulching**
- Apply 6-inch layer of mulch around tree (3 ft diameter circle)
- Materials: Dried grasses, straw, crop residues, wood chips
- Keep mulch 6 inches away from trunk (create donut, not volcano!)
- Why? Prevents trunk rot, reduces weed competition, retains moisture

**STEP 6: Staking** (for trees >4 ft tall)
- Drive stake 12-18 inches into ground, 6-12 inches from trunk
- Position stake on WINDWARD side (west side in Kurukshetra - prevailing wind direction)
- Tie tree to stake with soft material (cloth strips, rubber ties - NOT wire!)
- Tie should be loose "figure-8" pattern (allows some movement, prevents rubbing)
- Check ties monthly, loosen as tree grows (remove stake after 12-18 months)

**STEP 7: Tree Guard Installation**
- Install wire mesh cylinder OR bamboo cage around tree (3-4 ft height, 2 ft diameter)
- Secure guard so it can't be pushed over by animals
- Guard protects from:
  - Chickens/ducks scratching near trunk
  - Goats browsing (if you add goats Year 2+)
  - Wild animals (deer, rabbits)
  - Strong winds (provides physical support)

**STEP 8: Labeling** (IMPORTANT!)
- Label each tree with:
  - Species name
  - Variety (for fruits)
  - Planting date
  - Source (nursery)
- Use weatherproof labels (metal tags, plastic strips with permanent marker)
- Why? Track performance, know when to expect fruiting, identify for pruning

**TIME PER TREE:** 30-45 minutes (including mulching, staking, guard)

**TEAM PRODUCTIVITY:** 4 people working together can plant 8-12 fruit trees/day (including all steps)

### Technique 3: STEM CUTTING PLANTING (Gliricidia, Moringa - Direct Stick Method)

**This is FASTEST method - for mature Gliricidia/Moringa only**

**Cutting Specifications:**
- Length: 2-4 ft (60-120cm)
- Diameter: 2-4 inches (thick cuttings root better)
- Source: Healthy, disease-free mature tree (>2 years old)
- Cut type: Hardwood (woody stem, not soft green branch)

**Planting Steps:**

**STEP 1: Prepare Cutting**
- Cut stem at 45¬∞ angle (top and bottom) with sharp saw/machete
- Remove all leaves (except top 2-3 if soft tip present)
- Cutting can be planted IMMEDIATELY (no rooting hormone needed for Gliricidia/Moringa)

**STEP 2: Plant Cutting**
- Dig hole 12-18 inches deep (use crowbar or pointed stick in monsoon soft soil)
- Insert cutting 12-18 inches deep (bottom end down! - check cut angle)
- Firm soil around cutting (must be stable, not wobbly)
- Water lightly (3-5 liters)

**STEP 3: Mulch**
- Apply 2-4 inch mulch (keep mulch 3 inches from cutting stem)

**STEP 4: Wait**
- Roots form in 2-4 weeks (cutting may look dead - DON'T DISTURB!)
- New shoots emerge from top in 4-8 weeks
- Success rate: 70-90% (very high for Gliricidia/Moringa)

**TIME PER CUTTING:** 5 minutes!

**TEAM PRODUCTIVITY:** 4 people can plant 100-150 cuttings/day

**COST:** FREE (if source trees available) vs ‚Çπ20-40/sapling

**RECOMMENDATION:** Use cutting method for 50% of Gliricidia/Moringa (saves ‚Çπ2,000-5,000 per family!)

### Technique 4: SEED PLANTING (Subabul, Neem, Tamarind - Budget Option)

**For Maximum Savings:**

**Seed Specifications:**
- Source: Collect from mature trees (local trees = adapted to your climate)
- Timing: Collect seeds when pods/fruits mature (varies by species)
- Storage: Store in cool, dry place until planting

**Pre-Treatment (Species-Specific):**

**Subabul:**
- Soak seeds in hot water (80¬∞C) for 5 minutes
- Let cool, soak 12 hours in room-temp water
- This breaks seed coat dormancy

**Neem:**
- Remove flesh from fruit, wash seeds
- No pre-treatment needed (plant fresh)

**Moringa:**
- No pre-treatment (direct sow)

**Planting Steps:**

**OPTION A: Direct Sowing** (Plant seeds in final location)
- Dig 6-inch deep hole at planting location
- Plant 2-3 seeds per hole (insurance against low germination)
- Cover with 1-2 inches soil
- Water lightly
- Thin to 1 strongest seedling after 4-6 weeks

**OPTION B: Nursery Bed** (Grow seedlings first, transplant later - BETTER)
- Create nursery bed (4√ó8 ft raised bed, well-draining soil)
- Plant seeds 3-4 inches apart, 1-2 inches deep
- Water daily
- Transplant to final location when seedlings are 8-12 inches tall (8-12 weeks)
- Why better? Higher survival rate, easier to care for when small

**TIME PER SEED:** 2-3 minutes (direct sow) OR 20-30 minutes (transplant)

**GERMINATION TIME:** 2-4 weeks (varies by species)

**SUCCESS RATE:** 60-80% (lower than buying saplings, but FREE!)

**RECOMMENDATION:** Use seed method for "fill-in" trees (swale berms, Zone 4 timber) where some failure is acceptable

## PART 6: TROUBLESHOOTING & COMMON MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: **Planting Too Close** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Trees crowded, compete for light/water/nutrients
- Lower yields, increased disease (poor air circulation)
- Difficult to access for harvest/pruning

**Solution:**
- Follow spacing guidelines (above)
- If you discover trees too close in Year 1-2: TRANSPLANT or REMOVE weaker tree
  - Better to sacrifice 1 tree than have 2 weak trees

**How to Check:** 
- Mature spread of mango: 30-40 ft diameter
- At maturity, neighboring trees should BARELY touch (10-20% canopy overlap = okay)
- If you expect 50%+ overlap: TOO CLOSE

---

### Mistake 2: **Planting Too Deep** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Root collar buried below soil
- Results in: Root rot, stunted growth, eventual death

**Symptoms:**
- Tree looks stunted, yellowing leaves
- Inspection reveals trunk buried 4-6 inches below soil

**Solution (If caught early):**
- Carefully excavate soil around trunk (dig down 12 inches in 3 ft circle)
- Expose root flare (point where roots meet trunk - should be visible)
- Remove soil until root flare is 2-3 inches ABOVE ground level
- Do NOT disturb roots during excavation

**Prevention:**
- Always plant with root collar 2-3 inches ABOVE ground level
- Account for settling (tree will sink 1-2 inches as soil settles)

---

### Mistake 3: **Overwatering** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Waterlogged soil = root suffocation = root rot
- Especially common in heavy clay soil or poor-drainage areas

**Symptoms:**
- Yellowing leaves (starts with older leaves)
- Wilting despite wet soil (roots damaged, can't absorb water)
- Fungal growth around base, foul smell from soil

**Solution:**
- STOP watering immediately
- Improve drainage:
  - Dig channels to allow water to drain away
  - Add sand/gravel to soil around tree (improves drainage)
  - Plant on mound (6-12 inches raised above grade)
- Prune diseased roots (if root rot advanced)

**Prevention:**
- Check soil before watering (dig 3-4 inches, should be barely moist, not wet)
- In monsoon, usually NO supplemental watering needed
- Swale system should prevent waterlogging (water infiltrates through berm)

---

### Mistake 4: **Planting Wrong Season** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Planting in dry season (November-May) = very low survival rate
- Tree has no time to establish roots before extreme summer heat

**If You MUST Plant in Dry Season:**
- Only for potted saplings (bare-root = certain death)
- Water daily for first 2 weeks, then every 2-3 days for 3 months
- Mulch heavily (8-10 inches)
- Provide 50% shade cloth for first 2 months
- Expect 50-70% survival vs 85-95% monsoon planting

**BEST PRACTICE:** Wait for monsoon. It's worth it!

---

### Mistake 5: **No Mulch** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Soil dries out rapidly (especially sandy loam Kurukshetra soil)
- Weed competition extreme
- Soil temperature fluctuates (stresses roots)

**Impact on Survival:**
- Mulched trees: 85-95% survival
- Non-mulched trees: 50-70% survival (in Kurukshetra climate)

**Solution:**
- Mulch EVERY tree, 6-inch depth minimum
- Source: On-farm materials (free!) OR purchase straw (expensive)
- Replenish 2-3x/year (mulch breaks down = good for soil, but needs replacement)

---

### Mistake 6: **Ignoring Pests Early** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Small pest problem in Week 1-2 becomes major outbreak by Month 2-3
- Can kill tree or severely stunt growth

**Examples:**
- Termites: Kill 50% of unprotected saplings in some areas
- Borers: Can kill tree in 4-6 months if untreated
- Caterpillars: Can defoliate entire young tree in 1-2 weeks

**Solution:**
- Weekly inspections (non-negotiable!)
- Intervene EARLY (1 caterpillar = hand-pick; 100 caterpillars = spray)
- Keep neem oil on hand (5 liters per family minimum)

---

### Mistake 7: **Wrong Variety for Soil** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Planted acid-loving varieties (Alphonso mango, citrus) in alkaline Kurukshetra soil
- Trees survive but never thrive, minimal fruiting

**Symptoms:**
- Yellowing leaves (chlorosis) despite fertilization - iron deficiency in alkaline soil
- Slow growth (<6 inches/year)
- Poor fruit set

**Solution (If caught early):**
- Grafting: Cut tree to 18 inches, graft alkaline-tolerant variety
- Soil amendment: Sulfur + iron chelate (lowers pH temporarily, but expensive and temporary)

**Prevention:**
- ONLY plant varieties confirmed for pH 7.5-8.5 (see Variety Selection section above)
- When in doubt, ASK nursery: "Does this variety tolerate alkaline soil?"
