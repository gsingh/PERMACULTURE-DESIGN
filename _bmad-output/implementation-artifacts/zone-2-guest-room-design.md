## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Purpose**: Provide private guest accommodation in Zone 2 that integrates harmoniously with the food forest and animal systems while offering comfortable, peaceful retreat space for visitors.

**Location**: Eastern edge of Zone 2, near pond area  
**Size**: 250-300 sq ft main structure + 100 sq ft covered veranda  
**Capacity**: 2-3 guests  
**Key Features**: Privacy, natural cooling, food forest views, minimal environmental impact

**Design Philosophy**: The guest room embodies permaculture principles by:
- Using natural, locally-sourced materials
- Harvesting rainwater
- Integrating with surrounding food forest
- Providing wildlife observation opportunities
- Minimizing energy consumption through passive design
- Creating a demonstration of sustainable living

## 4. CLIMATE-RESPONSIVE FEATURES

### Passive Cooling (Summer: 35-45Â°C)

**Design Strategies:**

**1. Orientation & Shading:**
- [ ] East-facing windows: Morning sun only
- [ ] West-facing veranda: Blocks afternoon sun
- [ ] 3-4 foot roof overhang: Prevents direct sun on walls
- [ ] Deciduous trees on south/west: Summer shade, winter sun

**2. Natural Ventilation:**
- [ ] **Cross-ventilation**: Windows on opposite walls
- [ ] **Stack effect**: High vents near ceiling (hot air escapes)
- [ ] **Night ventilation**: Open windows at night (cool thermal mass)
- [ ] **Courtyard effect**: Veranda creates air movement zone

**3. Thermal Mass:**
- [ ] Adobe/earth walls: Absorb heat during day, release at night
- [ ] 12-inch wall thickness: Delays heat transfer by 8-10 hours
- [ ] Stone flooring: Stays cool naturally

**4. Evaporative Cooling:**
- [ ] Clay water pots (traditional surahi): Natural cooling
- [ ] Wet bamboo curtains on veranda (summer months)
- [ ] Small water feature near entrance: Cools incoming air

**5. Reflective Surfaces:**
- [ ] White/light-colored lime plaster: Reflects solar radiation
- [ ] Light-colored roof tiles
- [ ] Reflective roof coating (optional)

**Expected Results:**
- Indoor temperature: 4-7Â°C cooler than outside
- Comfortable even in 40Â°C+ heat with ceiling fan
- Minimal/no air conditioning needed

### Monsoon Protection (July-September)

**Water Management:**

**1. Roof Design:**
- [ ] Steep pitch (30-40 degrees): Rapid water runoff
- [ ] Large overhang: Protects walls from rain
- [ ] No flat areas: Prevents water pooling
- [ ] Sealed joints: No leaks

**2. Rainwater Harvesting:**
- [ ] Gutters on all roof edges
- [ ] Downpipes to collection tank
- [ ] **Tank size**: 2,000-3,000 liters (roof area ~300 sq ft)
  - Monsoon rainfall: 50-60 inches in 3 months
  - Collection potential: ~15,000 liters/season
  - Stored water: Supplements guest room needs year-round
- [ ] Overflow to swale/pond system
- [ ] First-flush diverter: Prevents contamination
- [ ] Cost: â‚¹20,000-30,000

**3. Drainage:**
- [ ] Grade slopes away from structure (2% minimum)
- [ ] French drains around foundation
- [ ] Greywater to banana circle (50 feet from structure)
- [ ] No water accumulation near building

**4. Moisture Protection:**
- [ ] Raised plinth: Above monsoon water level
- [ ] Damp-proof course: Below wall level
- [ ] Lime plaster: Breathable, prevents moisture buildup
- [ ] High ventilation: Prevents mold

### Wastewater Management

**Greywater System** (shower, sink, washing):

**Design:**
- [ ] Separate greywater from toilet (two drainage systems)
- [ ] Greywater â†’ 3-stage filtration:
  1. Grease trap (kitchen sink)
  2. Sand filter bed
  3. Mulch basin
- [ ] Final destination: **Banana circle** (50 feet from structure)

  - Central mulch pit

  - Plants filter water naturally
- [ ] Cost: â‚¹12,000-18,000

**Blackwater System** (toilet):

**Option A: Composting Toilet** (RECOMMENDED - most eco-friendly)
- No water needed (dry system)

- No smell with proper design
- Requires: Sawdust/carbon material, proper ventilation
- Cost: â‚¹25,000-40,000

**Option B: Septic System**

- Leach field 40+ feet from water sources
- Requires periodic pumping
- Cost: â‚¹30,000-50,000

**RECOMMENDATION**: Composting toilet - aligns with permaculture principles, educational for guests
