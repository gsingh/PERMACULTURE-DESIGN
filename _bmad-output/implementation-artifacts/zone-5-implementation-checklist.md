## PHASE 2: SHORT-TERM ACTIONS (MONTHS 4-12)

### 1. INVASIVE SPECIES REMOVAL CAMPAIGN

#### 1.1 Lantana Removal (Priority 1)
- [ ] **Month 4**: Remove Lantana from highest priority areas
  - [ ] Start with planned water point locations
  - [ ] Clear areas identified for pioneer tree planting
  - [ ] Focus on central core wilderness area first
- [ ] Remove entire root system (dig minimum 1 foot deep)
- [ ] Pile lantana stems for drying (composting later)
- [ ] Monitor cleared areas weekly for regrowth
- [ ] Re-remove any lantana sprouting within 2 weeks

#### 1.2 Parthenium Control (Priority 2)
- [ ] **Month 5-6**: Systematic Parthenium removal before flowering
- [ ] Hand-pull with roots intact (use gloves - causes skin allergies)
- [ ] Remove before seed formation (critical timing)
- [ ] Burn or deep bury pulled plants (prevent seed spread)
- [ ] Monitor and re-remove monthly during growing season
- [ ] Document coverage reduction over time

#### 1.3 Prosopis juliflora Management (Priority 3)
- [ ] **Month 6-8**: Identify all Prosopis trees
- [ ] Decide which trees to remove (based on location, size)
- [ ] Cut trees at base, remove stumps if possible
- [ ] Apply salt to stumps to prevent regrowth
- [ ] Use Prosopis wood for farm needs (firewood, tool handles)
- [ ] Monitor for root suckers and remove promptly

#### 1.4 Ongoing Invasive Monitoring
- [ ] Schedule monthly invasive species patrols
- [ ] Document removal progress with photos
- [ ] Track labor hours and methods used
- [ ] Adjust removal strategies based on effectiveness
- [ ] Create invasive species removal log

### 2. PIONEER SPECIES PLANTING

#### 2.1 Site Preparation for Planting
- [ ] **Month 6**: Prepare planting pits after invasive removal
- [ ] Dig pits: 2ft × 2ft × 2ft (60cm × 60cm × 60cm)
- [ ] Space pits 20-30 feet apart (nucleus planting strategy)
- [ ] Mix pit soil with compost/manure if available
- [ ] Create water-holding basins around each pit
- [ ] Mark pit locations on site map

#### 2.2 Pioneer Species Procurement
- [ ] **Month 6-7**: Source native pioneer species saplings:
  - [ ] **Neem (Azadirachta indica)**: 30-40 saplings
  - [ ] **Babul (Acacia nilotica)**: 25-35 saplings
  - [ ] **Khejri (Prosopis cineraria)**: 20-30 saplings
  - [ ] **Ber (Ziziphus mauritiana)**: 15-20 saplings
  - [ ] **Karonda (Carissa carandas)**: 15-20 saplings
- [ ] Source from local nurseries or forest department
- [ ] Verify species authenticity (avoid mistakes)
- [ ] Select healthy, 2-3 year old saplings
- [ ] Transport saplings carefully to site

#### 2.3 Monsoon Planting (Critical Timing)
- [ ] **Month 7-8 (June-July)**: Plant with first monsoon rains
- [ ] Plant saplings at appropriate depths (root collar level)
- [ ] Water thoroughly after planting
- [ ] Create mulch ring around each sapling (grass, leaves)
- [ ] Install simple tree guards if cattle threat exists
- [ ] Tag each tree with species name and planting date
- [ ] Document planting locations on map

#### 2.4 Post-Planting Care
- [ ] Water saplings weekly if monsoon gaps exceed 10 days
- [ ] Check tree guards and adjust as needed
- [ ] Remove competing weeds around saplings (3-foot radius)
- [ ] Monitor for pest/disease issues
- [ ] Replace any dead saplings within first 2 months
- [ ] Document survival rates monthly

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

### Success Factors
1. **Patience is paramount** - Wilderness development takes decades, not years
2. **Observe and respond** - Nature will guide; be willing to adjust
3. **Minimal intervention** - Less is often more in Zone 5
4. **Community support** - Long-term success requires local buy-in
5. **Documentation** - Record everything for learning and inspiration

### Budget Considerations
- **Keep costs minimal** - Zone 5 should be low-input
- **Prioritize labor over materials** - Use sweat equity
- **Source locally** - Native species from local nurseries/forest dept
- **Use on-farm resources** - Compost, mulch, seeds from property
- **Seek partnerships** - Collaborate with conservation groups for support

### Risk Management
- **Monitor boundary integrity constantly** - Trespass is primary threat
- **Act immediately on invasives** - Small problems become big fast
- **Protect from fire** - Especially critical in dry season
- **Maintain good neighbor relations** - They are your allies
- **Document everything** - Legal protection through records

### Celebration & Reflection
- **Celebrate milestones** - First monsoon, first bird nest, first natural seedling
- **Share successes** - Inspire others through your example
- **Learn from failures** - Every setback teaches valuable lessons
- **Honor the land** - This is sacred work of regeneration
- **Think generationally** - You're creating legacy for centuries
