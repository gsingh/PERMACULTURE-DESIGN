### 1.1 Breed Selection

### 1.4 Chicken Introduction Day (November 2026)

**TIMELINE:**

**DAY BEFORE (Evening):**
- Fill feeders with layer feed
- Fill waterers with fresh water
- Add 4-6 inches fresh bedding to coop floor
- Add straw to nesting boxes

**COLLECTION DAY (Morning):**
- Pick up chickens from hatchery/farm in early morning (cooler temperature)
- Transport: Ventilated crates, 5-6 birds per crate, 30-60 min max travel time
- Have helper with you (one person drives, one monitors birds)

**ARRIVAL (Morning 8-10 AM):**

**STEP 1: Initial Inspection (Before Release)**
- Check each bird for injuries, mites, illness
  - Eyes: Clear, bright (no discharge)
  - Nostrils: Clean, no mucus
  - Feathers: Smooth, no bald patches or mites
  - Feet: No swelling, cuts, scabs (bumblefoot)
  - Vent: Clean, no caked feces (indicates illness)
- Quarantine any sick-looking birds (separate coop area OR delay introduction)

**STEP 2: Release into Coop (NOT paddock yet!)**
- Carry birds gently into coop, place on floor
- Close pop door (confine to coop for 24-48 hours)
- Let birds explore, find feeders/waterers
- Expect: Confusion, pecking order establishment (some squabbling = normal)

**STEP 3: Monitor First 4 Hours**
- Check every 30-60 minutes
- Ensure all birds found water (dip beaks gently if they haven't drunk)
- Ensure all birds found feed
- Watch for extreme aggression (separate severe bullies)

**REST OF DAY 1:**
- Check at sunset (all birds should be on roosts)
- If birds on floor at dark, manually place on roosts (they'll learn)
- Close human door, ensure pop door closed
- Night patrol (optional): Check for predator activity around coop

---

**DAY 2-3: Coop Confinement**
- Keep birds in coop 24-48 hours
- Why? They learn "home" = coop (will return at dusk after free-ranging)
- Continue monitoring water, feed, behavior
- Collect any early eggs (some birds may lay immediately)

**DAY 4: First Free-Range Session**
- Open pop door at 8-9 AM (after sunrise)
- Birds will cautiously exit (don't force)
- Let them explore Paddock 1 for 2-3 hours
- Herd back into coop at 11 AM-12 PM (before heat of day)
- Close pop door

**DAY 5-7: Extended Free-Range**
- Open pop door at 8 AM
- Close at 6-7 PM (before dusk)
- Birds should return on own as dusk approaches (they learned coop = home)
- If stragglers, gently herd them in

**DAY 8+: Full Free-Range Routine**
- Pop door opens at dawn (automatic opener recommended)
- Birds free-range all day in assigned paddock
- Pop door closes at dusk (automatic OR manual)
- All birds should return on own by dusk

### 1.5 Daily Management Routine (Established System)

**MORNING ROUTINE (6-8 AM, 15-20 minutes):**

1. **Open Coop** (if not automatic)
   - Open pop door, let chickens exit to paddock
   - Observe: All birds healthy? Any limping, staying inside, lethargic?

2. **Collect Eggs**
   - Check all 6-8 nesting boxes
   - Collect eggs in basket
   - Note: Any broken eggs? Blood spots? Soft shells? (indicates issues)
   - Log: Number of eggs collected (track production trends)

3. **Check Feeders & Waterers**
   - Feed level: Refill if <1/3 full
   - Water: Refill if <1/2 full, check for cleanliness (no algae, debris)
   - Summer: Extra water (birds drink 2x more in heat)

4. **Quick Health Check**
   - Count birds (all present? or predator got one overnight?)
   - Behavior: Active, foraging normally? Or huddled, lethargic?
   - Droppings: Check floor under roosts - normal (firm, brown/green/white cap) vs diarrhea (watery, yellow, foul smell)

5. **Feed Kitchen Scraps** (Optional)
   - Vegetable peelings, fruit scraps, leftover rice/roti
   - Amount: 10-15% of diet (chickens mostly on forage + layer feed)
   - Avoid: Onions, raw beans, avocado, chocolate, salty foods

**AFTERNOON (Optional, 5 minutes):**
- Hot days (40¬∞C+): Check water, refill if needed
- Observe foraging behavior
- Move birds to next paddock (if rotation schedule says today)

**EVENING ROUTINE (6-7 PM, 10 minutes):**

1. **Herd Birds to Coop** (if needed)
   - Most will return on own by dusk
   - Stragglers: Gently herd with stick or arms outstretched
   - Do NOT chase aggressively (causes stress)

2. **Head Count**
   - All 20-25 birds back in coop?
   - Missing bird = predator (search area, check for remains, tighten security)

3. **Close Pop Door**
   - Secure latch (raccoon/mongoose proof - double latch recommended)
   - Check human door also locked

4. **Feed Check (Night)**
   - Refill feeders if empty (birds will eat early morning)

**WEEKLY ROUTINE (30-60 minutes):**

**Sunday Morning (or family-selected day):**

1. **Coop Deep Clean** (Every 2-4 weeks)
   - Remove ALL bedding from floor (compost it!)
   - Scrub dropping board (scraper + water)
   - Add 4-6 inches fresh bedding
   - Replace nesting box straw (if soiled)
   - Dust with diatomaceous earth OR wood ash (mite control)

2. **Health Inspection** (Every week)
   - Catch each bird (at night on roost = easiest)
   - Check for external parasites (mites, lice - tiny insects in feathers)
   - Check feet for bumblefoot (swollen pads, scabs)
   - Check vent for lice or prolapse
   - Treatment: Neem oil spray for mites, remove bumblefoot scabs, dust with wood ash

3. **Equipment Maintenance**
   - Wash feeders, waterers (scrub with brush, rinse well)
   - Check for rust, damage
   - Tighten any loose fencing
   - Check tree guards (adjust as trees grow)

**GRIT & CALCIUM:**

**Oyster Shell / Limestone Grit:**
- Purpose: Calcium for strong eggshells (chickens need 4-5g calcium/day when laying)
- Form: Crushed oyster shells or limestone (2-4mm particles)
- Amount: Free-choice (separate feeder, birds eat as needed)

- Purpose: Helps grind food in gizzard (chickens have no teeth!)

- Amount: Free-choice OR available in forage areas (chickens find naturally)

**EGG COLLECTION BEST PRACTICES:**

**Frequency:**
- **Twice daily** (morning + afternoon) in hot weather (prevents egg sitting in 40¬∞C heat, reduces cracking)
- **Once daily** (morning) in cool weather (Nov-Feb)

**Timing:**
- Most hens lay in morning (80% of eggs by 11 AM)
- Check again at 3-4 PM (catch remaining 20%)

**Handling:**
- Collect gently in basket lined with cloth/straw
- Avoid washing immediately (removes protective bloom - eggs stay fresh 2-3 weeks longer)
- Wash only if soiled (use water slightly warmer than egg, air dry, refrigerate)

**Storage:**
- Room temperature: 7-10 days (if unwashed, in cool season)
- Refrigerated: 4-6 weeks
- In India's climate: Refrigerate recommended (especially March-June heat)

---

**EGG QUALITY ISSUES (What They Mean):**

**Soft/Thin Shells:**
- Cause: Calcium deficiency OR too hot (hens pant, lose CO‚ÇÇ, affects shell formation)
- Solution: More oyster shell, add vitamin D (sunshine!), ensure ventilation

**Blood Spots:**
- Cause: Blood vessel rupture during egg formation (normal, harmless)
- Solution: None needed (occasional blood spot = normal; >10% of eggs = vitamin A deficiency)

**Double Yolks:**
- Cause: Young hen, two yolks released at once (common in first weeks of laying)
- Solution: None needed (considered special/lucky in India!)

**No Eggs (Sudden Drop):**
- Causes: Stress (predator scare, moved to new paddock), molt (annual feather replacement), illness, egg-eating habit
- Investigation: Check coop for broken eggs (egg-eating), check health, give birds 1-2 weeks to adjust

### 1.8 Health Management

**COMMON CHICKEN DISEASES (India):**

---

**1. Newcastle Disease (Ranikhet Disease)**

**Symptoms:**
- Sudden death (10-40% of flock)
- Gasping, coughing, nasal discharge
- Twisted neck, paralysis
- Green diarrhea

**Treatment:** NONE (viral, highly contagious)

**Prevention:**
- ‚úÖ **Vaccination CRITICAL!** F1 strain (day 7) + R2B/Lasota (6 weeks) + R2B booster (16 weeks, then every 6 months)
- Cost: ‚Çπ5-10 per bird per vaccination
- **DO NOT SKIP VACCINES** - Newcastle can wipe out entire flock in 3-7 days

---

**2. Fowl Pox (Chicken Pox)**

**Symptoms:**
- Wart-like lesions on comb, wattles, face
- In severe form: Lesions in mouth/throat (can't eat, death)

**Treatment:**
- Topical: Apply iodine/gentian violet to lesions
- Internal: None (viral, must run course)

**Prevention:**
- Vaccine: Available (once in lifetime, ‚Çπ10-15 per bird)
- Natural: Some recovery on own (4-6 weeks), develop immunity

---

**3. Coccidiosis (Intestinal Parasites)**

**Symptoms:**
- Bloody diarrhea
- Hunched posture, ruffled feathers
- Weight loss despite eating
- High mortality in young birds (20-50% if untreated)

**Treatment:**
- Sulfa drugs (Coccidiostat): Sulfaquinoxaline in water for 3-5 days
- Cost: ‚Çπ50-100 for treatment course (entire flock)

**Prevention:**
- Clean bedding (moisture + manure = coccidia breeding ground)
- Don't overcrowd
- Rotation (8-week paddock system breaks coccidia lifecycle!)

---

**4. Fowl Cholera**

**Symptoms:**
- Sudden death (chronic form: swollen wattles, joints)
- Greenish-yellow diarrhea
- Fever, loss of appetite

**Treatment:**
- Antibiotics: Tetracycline OR sulfadimidine (7-10 days)
- Consult vet for proper dosing

**Prevention:**
- Clean water (change daily!)
- Remove dead birds immediately (bacteria spread fast)
- Vaccination available (not routine in small flocks)

---

**5. External Parasites (Mites, Lice)**

**Symptoms:**
- Feather loss, scratching/pecking at feathers
- Irritation, restlessness at night (mites active at night)
- Pale combs (anemia from blood-sucking mites)
- Small insects visible in feathers, vent area

**Treatment:**
- **Neem Oil Spray:** 5ml neem oil + 1 liter water, spray birds (avoid eyes), repeat weekly for 3 weeks
- **Wood Ash Dust Bath:** Create dust bath area (2√ó2 ft shallow box, filled with fine soil + wood ash + sand), chickens self-treat by bathing
- **Diatomaceous Earth:** Dust coop, nesting boxes (kills mites mechanically)

**Prevention:**
- Weekly dust baths (chickens naturally control parasites)
- Clean bedding (mites live in coop, not on birds)
- Sunlight (UV kills mites)

### 2.7 Duck Health Management

**DUCKS ARE HARDIER THAN CHICKENS!**
- Lower disease rates (less prone to respiratory issues)

**COMMON DUCK DISEASES:**

**1. Duck Plague (Duck Virus Enteritis)**
- **Symptoms:** Bloody diarrhea, nasal discharge, sudden death
- **Treatment:** None (viral)
- **Prevention:** Vaccine available (‚Çπ15-20 per duck, annual) - RECOMMENDED for flocks >10

**2. Aspergillosis (Fungal Lung Infection)**
- **Symptoms:** Gasping, open-mouth breathing, weight loss

- **Prevention:** NEVER use moldy feed/bedding, keep duck house DRY (critical!)

**3. Bumblefoot (Foot Infection)**

- **Cause:** Cut on foot gets infected (more common in ducks than chickens - ducks walk more)
- **Treatment:** Soak foot in warm water + Epsom salt, apply turmeric paste, bandage
- **Prevention:** Keep forage area clear of sharp objects (thorns, broken glass, wire)

**4. External Parasites (Lice, Mites)**
- **Symptoms:** Feather loss, scratching
- **Treatment:** Neem oil spray (same as chickens)
- **Prevention:** Ducks self-maintain by bathing in pond (water naturally removes parasites)

**ORGANIC DUCK TREATMENT KIT:**
- Same as chickens (Neem oil, turmeric, wood ash, ACV)
- Additional: Epsom salt (for bumblefoot soaks) - ‚Çπ50-100/kg

**2 WEEKS BEFORE STOCKING:**

**‚òê Pond Preparation:**
- [ ] Check water level (should be 4-5 ft deep) - refill if needed
- [ ] Check water clarity (slight green tinge = good (algae), murky brown = too much sediment, crystal clear = not enough food)

**MORNING (Transport):**

**STEP 2: Water Quality Acclimation (30 minutes)**
- After temperature match, open bags/buckets
- Add 1-2 cups pond water every 10 minutes for 30 minutes
- Fish adjust to pond's pH, dissolved oxygen, mineral content

**STEP 3: Release**
- Gently pour fish from bag/bucket into pond (shallow area near edge)
- Observe: Fish should swim normally, not float or sink abnormally
- Expect: Fish will hide (under floating azolla, near pond edge) for first 2-3 days (normal fear response)

### 3.7 Fish Health & Water Quality

**COMMON FISH HEALTH ISSUES:**

**1. White Spot Disease (Ich)**
- **Symptoms:** White spots on fins/body (looks like salt grains)
- **Cause:** Parasite (common in stressed fish)
- **Treatment:** Salt bath (10g salt per liter water, 10-15 min daily for 3-5 days) OR raise water temperature to 30-32¬∞C (parasite dies)
- **Prevention:** Avoid stress (don't overstock, maintain water quality)

**2. Fin Rot**
- **Symptoms:** Frayed, discolored fins
- **Cause:** Bacterial infection (poor water quality)
- **Treatment:** Improve water quality, add salt (3g per liter pond water)
- **Prevention:** Don't overfeed (excess feed decays, pollutes water)

**3. Low Oxygen (Fish Gasping at Surface)**
- **Symptoms:** All fish at surface, gulping air
- **Cause:** Low dissolved oxygen (too many fish, no aeration, hot weather)
- **Treatment:** Splash water (aerate manually), reduce stocking density
- **Prevention:** Don't overstock, ducks swimming provides some aeration

**4. Ammonia Poisoning**
- **Symptoms:** Fish gasping, red gills, lethargic
- **Cause:** Too much feed OR dead fish decaying (ammonia spike)
- **Treatment:** Partial water change (replace 20-30% pond water with fresh), stop feeding 2-3 days
- **Prevention:** Don't overfeed, remove dead fish immediately

---

**WATER QUALITY MONITORING:**

**Weekly Checks (Visual):**
- **Color:** Slight green (algae) = good, brown/gray (excess sediment) = bad
- **Smell:** Earthy/fresh = good, sulfur/rotten = bad
- **Clarity:** See 12-18 inches below surface = good

**Monthly Checks (Test Kit - Optional but Recommended):**
- **pH:** 6.5-8.5 (tilapia tolerate wide range, 7.0-8.0 ideal)
- **Ammonia:** <0.5 ppm (>1 ppm toxic)
- **Nitrite:** <0.5 ppm (>1 ppm toxic)
- **Dissolved Oxygen:** >5 ppm (tilapia tolerate >3 ppm, but 5+ ideal)

**Test Kit Cost:** ‚Çπ500-1,500 (one-time, lasts 50-100 tests)

**RECOMMENDATION:**
- Year 1: Buy test kit (learn water quality)
- Year 2+: Test only if fish behave abnormally (experienced eye = adequate)

---

**WATER QUALITY MANAGEMENT:**

**If Water is Too Green (Algae Bloom):**
- Cause: Too much duck manure (over-fertilized)
- Solution: Reduce duck pond time (from 5 hours to 3 hours/day) OR partial water change (20%)

**If Water is Murky/Brown:**
- Cause: Too much sediment (ducks stirring bottom, heavy rain runoff)
- Solution: Let settle (don't disturb pond for 3-5 days), plant water plants (roots stabilize sediment)

**If Water Smells Bad:**
- Cause: Dead fish decaying OR overfeeding
- Solution: Search for dead fish (remove!), stop feeding 3-5 days, partial water change

**If Water Level Drops:**
- Cause: Evaporation (especially March-June, 1-2 inches/day possible!)
- Solution: Refill from swale overflow OR tubewell

## ‚ö†Ô∏è PART 5: TROUBLESHOOTING & COMMON MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: **Introducing Animals Too Early** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Trees not established (only 2-3 months old)
- Animals trample, damage young trees
- No tree guards installed yet

**Solution:**
- Wait minimum 3-4 months after tree planting
- Ensure tree guards on all trees <3 years old
- Start with chickens in paddock rotation (controlled access), not free-range

---

### Mistake 2: **Skipping Vaccines** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Newcastle disease outbreak (viral, spreads fast)
- Can kill 50-90% of flock in 1-2 weeks
- No treatment exists

**Solution:**
- Vaccinate ALL chickens: F1 (day 7) + R2B (6 weeks) + R2B booster (16 weeks, then every 6 months)
- Cost: ‚Çπ5-10/bird per shot (‚Çπ100-250 total for 20-25 birds per vaccination)
- **DO NOT SKIP** - this is cheap insurance

---

### Mistake 3: **Poor Predator Protection** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Snake enters coop at night, eats eggs OR kills bird
- Fox/dog breaks into coop, kills multiple birds
- Mongoose enters during day, attacks birds

**Solution:**
- Nightly lockup (pop door closed at dusk) - NON-NEGOTIABLE
- Double-latched doors (predators are smart!)
- Bury fencing 12 inches OR extend outward 12 inches at base
- Weekly perimeter patrol (look for holes, tracks, droppings)

**If Predator Gets In:**
- Identify predator (tracks, scat, kill pattern)
- Strengthen weak point immediately
- Set trap (if legal, safe) OR call wildlife control
- Don't wait - predators return to successful hunting sites

---

### Mistake 4: **Overfeeding Fish** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Excess feed sinks, decays, pollutes water
- Ammonia spike, fish suffocate
- Water smells bad, algae bloom

**Solution:**
- Feed only what fish eat in 5-10 minutes
- If food floating after 10 minutes = too much
- Skip feeding 1-2 days/week (fish eat natural pond food)

---

### Mistake 5: **Ignoring Early Illness Signs** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- 1 sick bird ignored, becomes 10 sick birds within week
- Disease spreads fast in flock

**Early Warning Signs:**
- Bird separated from flock
- Closed eyes, puffed feathers (cold even in warm weather)
- Diarrhea (watery, yellow, green, foul smell)
- Not eating/drinking
- Limping

**Solution:**
- Isolate sick bird immediately (separate coop/cage)
- Diagnose (observe symptoms, consult vet if needed)
- Treat (organic treatments OR antibiotics if bacterial)
- Monitor flock daily for 1-2 weeks (ensure not spreading)

---

### Mistake 6: **Letting Ducks Access Pond 24/7** ‚ùå

**Problem:**
- Ducks never return to duck house (sleep on pond floating platform or edge)
- Vulnerable to nighttime predators (foxes drink at pond = easy duck hunting)
- Eggs laid randomly around pond (hard to find, may be eaten by monitor lizards/rats)

**Solution:**
- Herd ducks to house EVERY evening at dusk
- Lock in overnight
- Release at dawn
- Ducks will adapt to routine (easier after first 2-3 weeks)
