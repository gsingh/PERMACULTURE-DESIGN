### Harvest Day Protocol

**Safety First:**
- [ ] All workers trained (chainsaw safety, felling direction)
- [ ] Safety gear: Hard hats, eye protection, gloves, steel-toe boots
- [ ] First aid kit on-site
- [ ] Clear work zone (no children, animals within 100 ft of felling)

**Felling Process:**
1. **Directional Notch:** Cut 45Â° notch on side tree should fall (toward open space)
2. **Back Cut:** Cut from opposite side, 2 inches above notch base (leave hinge wood)
3. **Escape Route:** Feller retreats at 45Â° angle as tree begins to fall
4. **Limb & Buck:** Remove branches (limbing), cut trunk into logs (bucking)

**Log Transport:**
- Tractor or truck to mill (if selling logs)
- OR portable sawmill to site (if on-farm processing)

**Stump Treatment:**
- For coppice species (Babul, Khejri): Cut at 3-5 ft height (leave stump for regrowth)
- For timber species (Shisham, Neem): Cut flush to ground, cover with soil (OR grind stump if desired)

### Risk 2: Market Price Fluctuation

**Scenario:** Shisham price drops from â‚¹1,20,000 to â‚¹80,000 per tree (33% decline)

- **Stagger harvest** (sell 2-3 trees/year, not 18 trees in 1 year - avoid flooding local market)
- **Value-added processing** (milled lumber, furniture worth MORE even if log prices drop)

**Impact:**

### Risk 3: Legal/Regulatory Changes

**Scenario:** Haryana bans private timber harvest (unlikely, but possible)

**Causes:**
- Environmental regulations (forest conservation)
- Political changes

**Mitigation:**
- **Keep records** (prove trees are farm-planted, not wild-harvested)
- **Advocacy** (join farmer associations, lobby for private timber rights)
- **Grandfather clause** (laws usually don't apply retroactively to existing trees)

**Probability:** <5% (private land timber rights generally protected in India)

### Risk 4: Family Conflict (Timber Revenue Distribution)

**Scenario:** Families disagree on harvest timing, revenue split, reinvestment

  - Example: "All harvest decisions require 75% family vote (3 out of 4)"
  - Revenue split: "Equal 25% shares, regardless of labor (all families contributed initial investment)"
- **Annual Meetings:** Review timber plan, adjust if needed
- **Independent Valuations:** Hire timber appraiser (avoids disputes over tree value)
- **Mediation Clause:** If conflict, neutral third-party mediator (not courts!)

**Recommendation:** **Draft timber revenue agreement NOW (Year 1), review every 5 years**
