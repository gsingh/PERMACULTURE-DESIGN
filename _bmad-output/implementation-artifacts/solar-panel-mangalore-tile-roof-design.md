#### Panel Orientation & Spacing

**Orientation:**
- **Landscape mode:** 2,278mm horizontal, 1,134mm vertical
- **Reason:** Fewer mounting rails needed, better structural distribution
- **Alternative (Portrait):** 1,134mm horizontal, 2,278mm vertical - NOT recommended (more rails needed, less efficient use of roof width)

**Spacing Between Panels:**

**Horizontal gap (side-by-side):**
- **Gap:** 10-15mm
- **Reason:** Thermal expansion (aluminum frames expand in heat)
- **Clamping:** Mid-clamps hold both edges with rubber gasket

**Vertical gap (row-to-row):**
- **Gap:** 10-15mm
- **Reason:** Same (thermal expansion + drainage)

**Clearance from roof edges:**
- **Ridge (top):** 300-500mm
  - Prevents shading from ridge vent
  - Allows hot air to escape (cooling)
  - Maintenance access
- **Eave (bottom):** 500-800mm
  - Water drainage from roof
  - Prevents panel overheating (airflow underneath)
  - Maintenance/cleaning access
  - Gutter clearance
- **Sides (east/west):** 300-500mm each
  - Maintenance access
  - Future expansion space
  - Wind load reduction (less edge exposure)

**Waterproofing Layers (Bottom to Top):**

   - Material: Butyl rubber tape (adhesive both sides)
   - Application: Applied to rafter top surface before hook placement
   - Function: Compresses under hook weight ‚Üí waterproof seal
   - Width: 50mm (covers hook footprint + 10mm margin)
   - Thickness: 1-2mm
   - Properties: Remains flexible, doesn't dry out, 20+ year life

2. **Layer 2: Tile Hook (Compression)**
   - Hook sits on butyl tape
   - Weight of panels + structure compresses hook into tape
   - Creates positive seal (water cannot penetrate)

3. **Layer 3: Tile Overlap (Original Drainage)**
   - Mangalore tiles installed with 75-100mm overlap
   - Water flows DOWN tile surface, then DOWN to next tile
   - Hook is UNDER tiles (never sees direct water)

4. **Layer 4: Silicone Sealant (Secondary Seal)**
   - Material: UV-resistant silicone (neutral cure, non-corrosive)
   - Application: Bead around hook perimeter (after hook installation)
   - Function: Fills any gaps, secondary barrier
   - Only where needed (don't over-seal - prevent drainage)

**Installation Process:**

1. Identify rafter location (tap tiles, measure from eave)
2. Carefully lift 2-3 tiles where hook will go
3. Clean rafter surface (remove dust, debris)
4. Apply butyl tape to rafter (50mm wide, centered on hook location)
5. Place rubber pad on butyl tape (prevents hook corrosion)
6. Slide tile hook under tiles, position on rafter
7. Lower tiles back over hook (hook now under tiles)
8. Apply small silicone bead around hook edges (where visible)
9. Check water drainage (pour water, verify flows around hook)

#### Weatherproofing of Electrical Connections

**Challenge:** All outdoor electrical connections must withstand rain, dust, UV, temperature extremes.

**Solution: MC4 Connectors + Proper Installation**

**MC4 Connectors (Industry Standard):**
- Manufacturer: Multi-Contact (St√§ubli) or equivalent (Amphenol, Phoenix Contact)
- Rating: 1000V DC, 20-30A
- Protection: IP67 (waterproof, dust-proof)
- Locking: Push-in, twist-lock (prevents accidental disconnect)
- Materials: UV-resistant plastic, silver-plated copper contacts
- Lifespan: 25+ years outdoors

**Installation Best Practices:**

1. **Strip Cable Correctly:**
   - Insulation strip length: 8-10mm (per MC4 spec)
   - Use wire stripper (not knife - prevents conductor damage)

2. **Crimp Connector Properly:**
   - Use MC4 crimping tool (dedicated tool, not pliers)
   - Crimp force: 2,000-3,000 N (tool applies correct force)
   - Pull test: Tug cable - should not come out

3. **Mate Connectors Fully:**
   - Insert male into female until "click"
   - Verify locked (cannot pull apart by hand)
   - Locking sleeve fully seated

4. **Form Drip Loops:**
   - Cable droops DOWN from connection point
   - Connection is at HIGHEST point of loop
   - Water runs away from connection

5. **UV Protection (Extra):**
   - Wrap connections with self-amalgamating tape (optional)
   - UV-resistant cable ties (secure loops)
   - Keep connections out of direct sun (under panels)

   - Clean rafter surface (brush off dust)
   - Apply tape, press firmly to rafter

3. **Hook Installation** (4-5 hours)
   - Carefully lift 2-3 tiles at first hook location
   - Place rubber pad on butyl tape
   - Slide hook under tiles, position on rafter
   - Lower tiles gently over hook
   - Verify hook is level and secure
   - Repeat for all 30-35 hooks

4. **Waterproofing Check** (1 hour)
   - Apply silicone sealant around visible hook edges
   - Verify tiles overlap correctly (no gaps)
   - Check drainage (pour water, verify flows correctly)

**Crew:** 2-3 workers (safety: never work alone on roof)

**Safety:** All workers wear harnesses, tied to roof anchors

---

**Day 2: Mounting Rail Installation (6-8 hours)**

**Process:**

1. **Rail Cutting** (1 hour)
   - Measure rail lengths (3.8-4.0m for 3-panel rows)
   - Cut rails to length (aluminum saw or angle grinder)
   - Deburr cut edges (file smooth)

2. **Rail Positioning** (2-3 hours)
   - Lay first rail on hooks (along top edge of bottom panel row)
   - Use laser level to verify horizontal (critical for panel alignment)
   - Mark bolt hole positions

3. **Rail Attachment** (3-4 hours)
   - Drill pilot holes in rails (if not pre-drilled)
   - Attach rails to hooks with bolts
   - Torque to specification: 15-20 Nm (use torque wrench)
   - Install all 6 rails (2 per panel row √ó 3 rows)
   - Apply anti-seize compound to bolts (prevent galvanic corrosion)

4. **Rail Alignment Check** (1 hour)
   - Verify all rails horizontal (laser level)
   - Check rail spacing (should match panel height + 10-20mm clearance)
   - Verify rails parallel (measure diagonal distances - should be equal)

**Crew:** 2-3 workers

---

**Day 3: Solar Panel Installation (6-8 hours)**

**Process:**

1. **Panel Delivery to Roof** (1-2 hours)
   - Use block and tackle OR ladder with panel hoist
   - 2-3 people per panel (panels are heavy: 27-30 kg, awkward size)
   - Stack panels on roof (flat, stable surface, away from edge)
   - NEVER stand panels on edge on tiles (will crack)

2. **Clamp Installation** (1 hour)
   - Attach end-clamps to rails (panel array corners)
   - Loosely install mid-clamps (will tighten after panels installed)

3. **Panel Placement** (3-4 hours)
   - Start with bottom row (Row 3: P7, P8, P9)
   - Lift panel, slide into end-clamps and mid-clamps
   - Verify 10-15mm gap from adjacent panels (thermal expansion)
   - Tighten mid-clamps (torque: 10-15 Nm - not too tight!)
   - Repeat for Row 2 (P4, P5, P6), then Row 1 (P1, P2, P3)
   - Install top end-clamps after last row

4. **Panel Alignment Check** (1 hour)
   - Verify all panels level (they should be - rails are level)
   - Check gaps consistent (10-15mm between all panels)
   - Verify clamps secure (wiggle test - panels should not move)

**Crew:** 3-4 workers (heavier panels require more hands)

**Safety:** Never walk on panels (will crack glass), step only on frames or rails

---

**Day 4: Electrical Wiring (6-8 hours)**

**Process:**

1. **Panel Interconnection** (2-3 hours)
   - Connect P1 (+) to P2 (-) using MC4 jumper cable
   - Connect P2 (+) to P3 (-), continue for all 9 panels (series string)
   - Verify polarity (positive to negative, never positive to positive)
   - Form drip loops (cables droop below connection points)
   - Secure cables to rails with plastic clips (every 30-40cm)

2. **String Cable Run to Roof Edge** (2 hours)
   - Route cables from P1 (-) and P9 (+) to roof edge
   - Run cables UNDER tiles (protected from UV)
   - Use cable clips to secure to battens (if accessible)
   - Exit at eave or through roof penetration (depends on design)

3. **Roof Penetration (if needed)** (1-2 hours)
   - Install roof flashing (under upper tiles, over lower tiles)
   - Cut hole for cable gland
   - Install cable gland, thread cables through
   - Tighten compression seal
   - Apply sealant around flashing perimeter

4. **Junction Box Installation** (1 hour)
   - Mount junction box on roof edge or near inverter
   - Connect string cables to junction box terminals
   - Install DC disconnect switch (if in junction box)
   - Label cables (positive, negative, string ID)

5. **Conduit Run to Inverter** (1-2 hours)
   - Install conduit from junction box to inverter location
   - Pull cables through conduit
   - Leave service loops at both ends (1-2 feet extra cable)

**Crew:** 2 workers (1 electrician + 1 helper)

**Safety:** All electrical work done with panels COVERED (cardboard or tarp - prevents voltage generation)

---

**Day 5: Testing, Waterproofing Inspection, Cleanup (4-6 hours)**

**Process:**

1. **Electrical Testing** (2-3 hours)
   - **Open Circuit Voltage (Voc):** Measure with multimeter
     - Expected: 54-55V per panel √ó 9 = 486-495V (full string)
     - Actual should be within 5% of expected
   - **Short Circuit Current (Isc):** Measure with DC clamp meter
     - Expected: 12.5-13A (single string)
     - Test in full sun (10am-2pm)
   - **Insulation Resistance:** Test with megohmmeter
     - Positive to ground: >1 megohm (good insulation)
     - Negative to ground: >1 megohm
   - **Polarity Check:** Verify positive and negative correct
   - **String Performance:** Measure Vmp and Imp (should match panel specs)

2. **Waterproofing Inspection** (1 hour)
   - Inspect all tile hooks (silicone seal complete?)
   - Check roof penetrations (flashing sealed?)
   - Inspect cable glands (compression tight?)
   - Pour water test (check drainage around hooks)

3. **Mechanical Inspection** (1 hour)
   - Re-check all bolts (torque wrench - verify tightness)
   - Inspect panel clamps (all secure?)
   - Check grounding connections (continuity test)
   - Verify no sharp edges (cable chafing risk)

4. **Cleanup** (1 hour)
   - Remove all tools, materials from roof
   - Sweep roof (remove fasteners, debris)
   - Clean panels (water + soft brush)
   - Remove scaffolding, ladders
   - Restore landscaping

**Crew:** 2 workers (electrician + helper)

### Tile Replacement Procedure (If Tile Breaks Under Panels)

**Scenario:** A tile cracks and needs replacement. It's under installed solar panels.

**Process (1-2 hours):**

1. **Identify Affected Tile:**
   - Note tile location (row, column from eave)

2. **Partial Panel Removal:**

   - Slide panel back to original position
   - Re-tighten clamps (torque: 10-15 Nm)

## ‚ö†Ô∏è SAFETY & RISK MITIGATION

### Critical Safety Considerations

#### **Electrical Safety:**

**HIGH VOLTAGE WARNING:**
- Solar panels generate voltage IN DAYLIGHT (even cloudy)
- 9-panel string = **400+ volts DC** (can be lethal)
- DC is MORE dangerous than AC (doesn't "let go")

**Safety Protocols:**

**1. Never Work on Live System:**
- ALWAYS cover panels with opaque material (cardboard, tarp) before touching wires
- Covering panels = no light = no voltage generation
- Use DC disconnect switch (isolate panels from inverter)

**2. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):**
- Insulated gloves (rated for 1000V DC)
- Safety glasses (arc flash protection)
- Non-conductive footwear (rubber soles)
- Long sleeves (arc flash, sun protection)

**3. Lockout/Tagout:**
- Lock DC disconnect in OFF position during maintenance
- Hang warning tag: "DANGER: DO NOT OPERATE"
- Keep key in your pocket (no one can turn on while you work)

**4. Test Before Touch:**
- Use multimeter to verify zero voltage before touching wires
- Test positive to negative: Should read 0V (if panels covered)
- Test positive to ground: Should read 0V
- Test negative to ground: Should read 0V

---

#### **Fall Protection (Roof Work):**

**Fall Risk:**
- 30¬∞ slope = significant fall risk
- Mangalore tiles are SLIPPERY when wet
- Average roof height: 10-15 feet (serious injury risk)

**Fall Protection Measures:**

**1. Personal Fall Arrest System:**
- Full-body harness (NOT just waist belt)
- Shock-absorbing lanyard (6 feet max)
- Roof anchor points (temporary or permanent)
- Properly fitted (snug, not loose)

**2. Roof Ladder:**
- Ladder hooks over ridge, distributes weight across many tiles
- Prevents tile cracking from concentrated foot pressure
- Allows worker to climb without stepping on fragile tiles

**3. Guardrails or Barriers:**
- Temporary guardrail at roof edge (prevents fall off edge)
- Standard: 42 inches high, mid-rail at 21 inches, toe board

**4. Safe Work Practices:**
- NEVER work alone on roof (buddy system - one on ground)
- NEVER work in rain, fog, or high wind (too dangerous)
- NEVER work on wet tiles (extremely slippery)
- Keep tools tethered (prevent dropping on people below)
- Establish ground safety zone (no one below workers)

---

#### **Fire Safety:**

**Fire Risk from Solar Panels:**
- DC arc faults (loose connection sparks)
- Overheating (shading, defective panel)
- Lightning strike (direct hit or surge)
- Installer error (incorrect wiring, undersized cables)

**Fire Prevention:**

**1. Proper Installation:**
- Use correct cable sizes (prevents overheating)
- Torque all connections to spec (prevents arcing)
- Install arc-fault circuit interrupters (AFCI) - detects arcing
- Use fire-rated cable glands at roof penetrations

**2. Clearance from Combustibles:**
- Maintain 6-inch clearance from wood trim, fascia
- Route cables through conduit (not directly on wood)
- Use metal junction boxes (not plastic, near wood)

**3. Rapid Shutdown:**
- Install DC disconnect switch (accessible, labeled)
- Modern inverters have rapid shutdown (turns off array in emergency)
- Train families on emergency shutdown procedure

**4. Fire Extinguishers:**
- Class C extinguisher (electrical fires) near inverter
- DO NOT use water on electrical fire (electrocution risk)

---

#### **Structural Failure:**

**Risk:** Roof collapse under panel weight (if inadequately sized)

**Prevention:**
- ‚úÖ **Hire structural engineer** (non-negotiable)
- ‚úÖ Verify rafter size, spacing, condition
- ‚úÖ Reinforce if needed (additional rafters, bracing)
- ‚úÖ Inspect annually for sagging (deflection measurement)

---

#### **Weather Damage:**

**High Wind:**
- Risk: Panels blow off roof (inadequate clamping)
- Prevention: Use high-quality clamps, torque to spec, annual re-torque

**Hail:**
- Risk: Panel glass cracks (hail impact)
- Prevention: Buy panels with hail rating (25mm at 23 m/s), verify insurance coverage

**Lightning:**
- Risk: Direct strike destroys panels, inverter, batteries
- Prevention: Grounding system, SPD devices, lightning rods (if building tall/isolated)
