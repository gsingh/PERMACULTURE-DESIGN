- **Why**: Excellent egg production, heat-tolerant, disease-resistant, foraging efficiency

## 4. ROTATIONAL GRAZING/FORAGING SYSTEM

### Chicken Rotation Protocol (8-Week Cycle)

**Week 1-2: Paddock 1**
- Move portable electric netting to Paddock 1
- Chickens scratch, forage, fertilize
- Trees protected with individual guards
- Daily egg collection from coop (adjacent)
- Water stations: 3 locations

**Week 3-4: Paddock 2**
- Shift netting to Paddock 2
- Paddock 1 begins rest period (grass/weed regrowth)
- Monitor manure distribution
- Adjust tree protection as needed

**Week 5-6: Paddock 3**
- Continue rotation
- Paddocks 1-2 actively regenerating
- Scout for pests/issues

**Week 7-8: Paddock 4**
- Final rotation
- Evaluate Paddock 1 readiness (6 weeks rest complete)
- Cycle repeats

**Benefits of 8-Week Cycle**:
- âœ… Breaks parasite lifecycle (6-week rest minimum)
- âœ… Prevents overgrazing/scratching damage
- âœ… Even manure distribution
- âœ… Improved soil health across entire Zone 2
- âœ… Chickens always have fresh forage

---

### Duck Rotation Protocol (6-Week Cycle)

**Week 1-3: Forage Zone 1** (Western section)
- Ducks spend mornings foraging in Zone 1
- Afternoons at pond (swimming, feeding on Azolla)
- Evenings return to duck house (near pond)
- Focus: Pest control around young fruit trees

**Week 4-6: Forage Zone 2** (Center-East section)
- Shift to fresh foraging area
- Zone 1 rests (less pressure than chickens, 3-week rest adequate)
- Continue daily pond access
- Heavy pest reduction

**Daily Schedule** (Ducks):
- **6-7 AM**: Release from duck house â†’ Forage zone
- **11 AM-3 PM**: Pond access (swimming, feeding, cooling)
- **3-6 PM**: Return to forage zone
- **6-7 PM**: Herd to duck house (lock in for predator protection)

**Benefits**:
- âœ… Outstanding pest control (slugs, snails decimated)
- âœ… Fertilization while foraging
- âœ… Pond integration (nutrient cycling)
- âœ… Fresh water for ducks daily
- âœ… Less destructive than chickens (no heavy scratching)

## 6. ANIMAL INTEGRATION WITH FOOD FOREST

### Food Forest Design (Zone 2)

**Tree Planting Along Swales** (from your existing plan):
- **Swale 1 berm**: Pioneer nitrogen fixers (Gliricidia, Subabul) - 40-50 trees
- **Swale 2 berm**: Fruit trees (Mango, Jamun, Guava) + nitrogen fixers - 40-50 trees
- **Between swales**: Scattered fruit trees, mid-story species - 30-40 trees
- **TOTAL Zone 2 trees**: 110-140 trees

**Animal-Tree Integration Strategies**:

#### **Chickens + Trees**

**Benefits**:
- âœ… **Pest control**: Chickens eat beetles, grubs, grasshoppers attacking young trees
- âœ… **Fertilization**: High-nitrogen manure accelerates tree growth
- âœ… **Weed suppression**: Scratching controls weeds around tree bases
- âœ… **Soil aeration**: Scratching improves soil structure

**Challenges**:
- âš ï¸ **Bark damage**: Chickens scratch tree bark (protect young trees)
- âš ï¸ **Root exposure**: Excessive scratching can expose roots

**Solutions**:
- **Tree guards**: 3-foot diameter wire mesh circles around each tree (first 3-5 years)
- **Timing**: Rotate chickens through areas BEFORE tree planting or AFTER trees established (5+ feet tall)
- **Density management**: No more than 1 chicken per 100 sq ft to prevent overgrazing

---

#### **Ducks + Trees**

**Benefits**:
- âœ… **SUPERIOR pest control**: Ducks eat slugs, snails, insects WITHOUT scratching
- âœ… **Fertilization**: Duck manure excellent for trees
- âœ… **Weed management**: Ducks eat weed seeds, tender shoots
- âœ… **No bark damage**: Ducks don't scratch like chickens

**Challenges**:
- âš ï¸ **Tender greens**: Ducks love eating tender leaves (protect very young trees)

**Solutions**:
- **Indian Runner ducks**: Best for pest control, less destructive to plants
- **Timing**: Introduce ducks to areas with established trees (2+ years old)
- **Temporary fencing**: Protect very young saplings until 3 feet tall

### Egg Distribution Protocol

- Remaining 70% split equally among all 4 families

**Option C: Purchase System**
- All eggs go to communal "egg bank"
