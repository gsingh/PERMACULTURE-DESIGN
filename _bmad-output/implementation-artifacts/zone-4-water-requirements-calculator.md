## WATER EFFICIENCY TIPS (Maximize Every Drop!)

### Soil Water Retention Strategies

**1. Mulching (CRITICAL!):**
- **Thickness:** 4-6 inches around each tree
- **Material:** Straw, dried leaves, wood chips, grass clippings
- **Effect:** 40-50% reduction in evaporation
- **Cost:** â‚¹0-5,000 (free if collected on-farm, purchase if needed)
- **Replenish:** 2x per year (monsoon, spring)

**2. Compost Addition (Done in pit preparation):**
- **Organic matter:** 20-30% compost in pits
- **Effect:** 3-5x better water retention vs. alkaline soil alone
- **Benefit:** Less frequent watering needed

**3. Biochar (Optional, Advanced):**
- **Application:** 10-20% biochar in pits (if available)
- **Effect:** 2-3x water holding capacity
- **Cost:** â‚¹5,000-10,000 for 316 pits (if purchased)
- **DIY:** Make biochar from farm prunings (free, labor-intensive)

**4. Cover Crops (Row 5 Ground Layer):**
- **Effect:** Living mulch, prevents soil evaporation
- **Species:** Native grasses, wildflowers (planted in Row 5)
- **Benefit:** Reduces water need for trees (microclimate effect)

---

### Watering Technique Optimization

**1. Deep Watering (Not Frequent Shallow):**
- **Method:** 20-30 liters SLOW delivery (over 30-60 min, drip ideal)
- **Effect:** Encourages DEEP roots (drought-resistant trees)
- **Avoid:** Frequent light watering (encourages shallow roots, water stress)

**2. Watering Basin (Critical for Manual):**
- **Design:** 3 ft diameter, 3-4 inch deep rim around each tree
- **Effect:** Holds water (prevents runoff), directs to roots
- **Maintenance:** Re-form basin after rain (erosion flattens rim)

**3. Timing (Reduce Evaporation):**
- **BEST:** Early morning (6-9 AM) - cool, low evaporation, trees hydrated for day
- **AVOID:** Midday (10 AM-4 PM) - 30-50% evaporation loss!
- **OK:** Evening (5-7 PM) - but wet foliage overnight = fungal risk

**4. Targeted Watering (Drip Root Zone):**
- **Drip emitters:** 12-18 inches from trunk (root zone)
- **Avoid:** Wetting trunk (rot risk), wetting leaves (fungal disease)

---

### Monitoring Soil Moisture (Avoid Over/Under-Watering)

**Simple Hand-Feel Test:**
- **Dig 6 inches** next to tree (outside watering basin)
- **Squeeze soil:**
  - **Too DRY:** Crumbles, dusty (WATER IMMEDIATELY!)
  - **PERFECT:** Forms ball, slightly moist, doesn't drip (IDEAL)
  - **Too WET:** Muddy, water drips from fist (SKIP watering, wait 3-5 days)

**Frequency:**
- Check 5-10 random trees weekly (represent Row 1-4)
- Adjust watering schedule based on results

**Soil Moisture Meter (Optional, â‚¹500-1,500):**
- Insert probe 6-12 inches deep
- Reads: DRY / MOIST / WET
- More accurate than hand-feel
