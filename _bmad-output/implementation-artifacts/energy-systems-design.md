## ‚ö° SOLAR POWER SYSTEM

### Option 1: Grid-Tied Solar PV (RECOMMENDED)

**Concept:** Solar panels generate electricity during daytime, excess fed to grid (net metering), grid supplies power at night/cloudy days.

**Advantages:**
- No battery costs (grid acts as storage)
- Maximum efficiency (no battery losses)
- Government net metering policies (sell excess power)
- Lower maintenance (no battery replacement)
- 40% subsidy available (residential rooftop schemes)

**Disadvantages:**
- No power during grid outages (unless hybrid system)
- Dependent on grid stability (DHBVN reliability in Haryana)

### Option 2: Hybrid Solar PV (Grid-Tied + Battery Backup)

**Concept:** Solar + batteries + grid, provides power during outages, maximizes self-consumption.

**Advantages:**
- Power during grid outages (essential loads continue)
- Store excess solar for evening use (reduce grid dependency)
- Maximize self-consumption (less grid export)
- Energy independence (reduces reliance on DHBVN)

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher upfront cost (batteries expensive)
- Battery replacement (5-10 years, ‚Çπ1,50,000-2,50,000)
- Maintenance (battery monitoring, equalization)
- Lower subsidy eligibility (40% only on panel/inverter, not batteries)

### Operation & Maintenance

**Daily Tasks (15 minutes):**
1. Mix fresh manure with equal water (bucket in inlet chamber)
2. Feed slurry into inlet (6-8 kg manure + water daily)
3. Collect overflow slurry from outlet (bio-fertilizer)
4. Check gas pressure (manometer reading 4-8 inches water column)

**Weekly Tasks:**
1. Stir digester (optional, improves gas production)
   - Use long bamboo pole through manhole
   - Break surface scum layer
2. Clean inlet/outlet chambers (remove debris)

**Yearly Tasks:**
1. Full digester cleanout (remove settled solids)
   - Empty digester (pump out slurry)
   - Scrub walls, inspect for cracks
   - Refill, restart with 20% old slurry (inoculant)
2. Check gas line for leaks (soapy water test)
3. Service biogas stove (clean burners, replace seals)

**Maintenance Cost:** ‚Çπ2,000-5,000 per year

---

### Biogas Production Optimization

**Temperature Management:**
- **Optimal:** 30-35¬∞C (mesophilic bacteria)
- **Challenge:** Kurukshetra winters (5-10¬∞C) slow production
- **Solution:**
  - Underground placement (insulation, 15-20¬∞C stable)
  - Black-painted water tank on top (solar heating in winter)
  - Compost ring around digester (heat from decomposition)

**C:N Ratio:**
- **Target:** 20-30:1 (carbon to nitrogen)
- **Chicken/Duck Manure:** ~7-8:1 (very nitrogen-rich)
- **Solution:** Mix with carbon-rich materials
  - Add straw, dry leaves, shredded paper (1:1 ratio with manure)
  - This also increases gas yield (more feedstock)

**pH Management:**
- **Target:** 6.8-7.5 (neutral to slightly alkaline)
- **Monitor:** Monthly pH test (litmus paper or meter)
- **Correction:**
  - Too acidic (<6.5): Add lime powder (calcium carbonate)
  - Too alkaline (>8.0): Reduce ash/alkaline inputs

## üö® TROUBLESHOOTING

### Solar PV Common Issues

**Problem: Low Generation (Below Expected)**
- **Causes:** Panel soiling, shading, inverter issues
- **Solutions:** Clean panels, trim shade-causing trees, check inverter display/app

**Problem: No Power During Outage (Grid-Tied)**
- **Cause:** Grid-tied inverters shut down (safety feature)
- **Solution:** Upgrade to hybrid system with battery backup, or install separate off-grid system for essential loads

**Problem: Net Meter Not Recording Correctly**
- **Cause:** Meter malfunction, improper installation
- **Solution:** Contact DHBVN, request meter check/replacement

---

### Biogas Common Issues

**Problem: No Gas Production**
- **Causes:** Cold temperature, insufficient feedstock, pH imbalance, air leak
- **Solutions:**
  - Winter: Insulate digester, reduce water ratio
  - Check pH: Adjust with lime (if acidic) or reduce alkaline inputs
  - Leak test: Soapy water on all connections, seal leaks

**Problem: Low Gas Pressure**
- **Causes:** Insufficient slurry, gas leaks, low temperature
- **Solutions:** Increase daily feeding, repair leaks, insulate digester

**Problem: Foul Smell from Outlet**
- **Cause:** Normal (anaerobic process produces odor)
- **Solution:** Locate outlet away from living areas, plant aromatic shrubs around digester
