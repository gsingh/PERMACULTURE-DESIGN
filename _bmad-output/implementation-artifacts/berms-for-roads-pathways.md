**Equipment Needed:**
- Small excavator or tractor with box blade
- Compaction equipment (roller or plate compactor)
- Dump truck for gravel delivery

**Construction Steps:**

1. **Clear and level road path** (Day 1-2)
   - Remove vegetation (save for compost)
   - Rough grading for drainage
   - Create center crown (6 inches higher than edges)

2. **Create berms** (Day 2-3)
   - Excavate shallow channels on both sides (6-12 inches deep)
   - Use excavated soil + additional soil to build berms
   - Shape berms: 2-3 ft high, 4-6 ft wide at base
   - Compact lightly (but not too much - roots need to penetrate)

3. **Road surface** (Day 4-5)
   - Lay base layer: 4-6 inches gravel, compact well
   - Lay top layer: 2-3 inches fine gravel, compact
   - Ensure crown is maintained (center highest)
   - Test drainage with water

4. **Berm finishing** (Day 6-7)
   - Top with 3-4 inches compost/topsoil
   - Create shallow basins around tree planting spots
   - Mulch heavily (4-6 inches) except tree spots

5. **Tree planting** (Week 3)
   - Dig 2x2x2 feet holes in berms (15-20 ft spacing)
   - Mix native soil + compost + amendments
   - Plant saplings
   - Heavy mulch around each tree
   - Water deeply
   - Stake if needed

## âš ï¸ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### âŒ Mistake 1: Making Roads Too Wide
**Problem:** Wastes space, more impervious surface, less room for berms
**Solution:** Minimum width needed: 12-15 ft for vehicles, 4-6 ft for paths

### âŒ Mistake 2: Not Creating Proper Crown
**Problem:** Water pools on road, creates mud, damages surface
**Solution:** Center always 4-6 inches higher than edges

### âŒ Mistake 3: Planting Trees Too Close to Road
**Problem:** Roots damage road surface, branches interfere with vehicles
**Solution:** Keep 2-3 feet clear space between road and berm

### âŒ Mistake 4: Using Wrong Tree Species
**Problem:** Fast-growing but weak trees fall on road, or wrong root systems
**Solution:** Choose appropriate species (see recommendations above)

### âŒ Mistake 5: Building Berms Too Steep
**Problem:** Erodes quickly, hard to plant, looks unnatural
**Solution:** Gentle slopes (3:1 ratio), stable and plantable

### âŒ Mistake 6: Forgetting Overflow Management
**Problem:** Berms overflow and erode during heavy rain
**Solution:** Spillways every 100-150 feet, rock-lined if needed

### âŒ Mistake 7: No Mulch
**Problem:** Exposed soil erodes, weeds take over, moisture lost
**Solution:** Heavy mulch (4-6 inches) on all berms, replenish annually
