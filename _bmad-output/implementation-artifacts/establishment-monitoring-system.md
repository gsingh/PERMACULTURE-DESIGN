## üìã EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This system monitors the health and success of all installed systems (plants, animals, water, soil) during the critical establishment phase. Early detection of issues allows quick intervention, dramatically improving long-term success rates.

**Key Monitoring Areas:**
1. **Tree Establishment** (562-760 trees across all zones)
2. **Animal Health & Production** (chickens, ducks, fish)
3. **Water System Performance** (swales, pond, irrigation)
4. **Soil Health** (fertility, structure, biology)
5. **Pest & Disease Management** (early warning system)

**Monitoring Frequency:**
- **Daily:** Animal health, critical systems (first 3 months)
- **Weekly:** Tree watering needs, pest/disease checks
- **Monthly:** Growth measurements, system audits
- **Seasonal:** Comprehensive reviews, adaptation planning

   - Surface mulch thickness
   - Compost integration
   - Leaf litter accumulation
   - Color (dark brown/black = high OM)

**Earthworm Count Protocol:**
- Dig 1 cubic foot hole (12√ó12√ó12 inches)
- Count all earthworms
- Record size (small, medium, large)
- **Scoring:**
  - 0-2 worms: Poor soil health
  - 3-7 worms: Fair soil health
  - 8-15 worms: Good soil health

## üêõ PEST & DISEASE EARLY WARNING

### Weekly Pest Scouting (1 hour)

**Target Pests (Kurukshetra Region):**

**Trees:**
- Fruit flies (mango, guava)
- Aphids (citrus, stone fruits)
- Scale insects (most species)
- Leaf miners (various)
- Caterpillars (defoliators)

**Vegetables (Zone 1):**
- Whiteflies (tomatoes, peppers)
- Aphids (brassicas, legumes)
- Cutworms (young transplants)
- Leaf beetles (various)

**Action Thresholds:**
| Pest | Low (Monitor) | Moderate (Soft Control) | High (Aggressive Control) |
|------|---------------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Fruit flies | 1-2 per trap/week | 3-10 per trap/week | >10 per trap/week |
| Aphids | <10 per leaf | 10-50 per leaf | >50 per leaf, curling |
| Caterpillars | 1-2 per tree | 3-10 per tree | >10 per tree, defoliation |

**Organic Control Methods:**
1. **Soft Control:** Neem oil spray, beneficial insect release
2. **Moderate Control:** Soap spray, manual removal, traps
3. **Aggressive Control:** Bt spray (caterpillars), pyrethrin (last resort)

---

### Disease Monitoring (Bi-Weekly)

**Common Diseases:**
- Powdery mildew (cucurbits, grapes) - monsoon
- Fungal leaf spots (various) - monsoon
- Root rot (poorly drained areas) - monsoon
- Bacterial wilt (solanaceous crops) - summer

**Disease Severity Scale:**
- **1-2:** Isolated leaves, <5% plant affected
- **3-5:** Multiple leaves, 5-25% plant affected
- **6-8:** Whole plant affected, spreading to neighbors
- **9-10:** Severe infection, plant likely to die

**Prevention Strategies:**
- Adequate plant spacing (air circulation)
- Avoid overhead watering (drip irrigation)
- Mulch to prevent soil splash
- Remove infected plant material
- Crop rotation (Zone 1 gardens)

## üö® INTERVENTION PROTOCOLS

### Emergency Response (24-48 Hour Action)

**Tree Death Spike (>10% Mortality in 1 Week):**
1. Identify cause (drought, disease, pests, herbicide drift)
2. Isolate affected area (quarantine if disease)
3. Consult permaculture expert or extension agent
4. Implement emergency watering or treatment
5. Document incident thoroughly (photos, notes)

**Animal Health Crisis (Multiple Sick Birds/Fish Deaths):**
1. Isolate sick animals immediately
2. Call veterinarian (same day)
3. Test water/feed for contamination
4. Halt egg distribution until cleared
5. Deep clean coops/pond if disease confirmed

**Water System Failure (Swale Breach, Pond Leak):**
1. Stop irrigation if tubewell issue
2. Repair breach with sandbags (temporary)
3. Schedule contractor for permanent fix (1 week max)
4. Reroute water around damaged area
5. Hand-water critical trees until repair complete
