## 9. CONFLICT RESOLUTION

### 9.1 Conflict is Normal

**We acknowledge:**
- Disagreements will happen
- Conflict is not failure - it's an opportunity to clarify and grow
- We commit to resolving conflicts respectfully and promptly

### 9.2 Conflict Resolution Process (ESCALATION STEPS)

**STEP 1: Direct Conversation (1-on-1)**
- If two families have a conflict, they talk directly first
- Goal: Understand each other's perspectives, find solution
- Timeline: Within _____ days of issue arising

**STEP 2: Mediated Conversation (with 3rd party)**
- If Step 1 doesn't resolve, request mediation
- Mediator: [ ] One of the other two families [ ] Neutral outsider: _________________________
- Mediator facilitates conversation, doesn't impose solution
- Timeline: Within _____ days of requesting mediation

**STEP 3: Full Group Discussion**
- If Step 2 doesn't resolve, bring to full group meeting
- All 4 families discuss, seek consensus solution
- May require multiple meetings
- Timeline: Discussed at next regular meeting, or call special meeting within _____ days

**STEP 4: External Mediation**
- If Step 3 doesn't resolve, hire professional mediator
- Cost: Split equally among all 4 families
- Mediator: _________________________ (if pre-identified) or [ ] Find together
- Timeline: Within _____ days of Step 3 failing

**STEP 5: Arbitration or Exit**
- If Step 4 doesn't resolve, options are:
  - [ ] Binding arbitration (arbitrator makes decision we must follow)
  - [ ] One party exits the community (see Section 10)
  - [ ] Dissolve entire project (nuclear option)

### 9.3 Conflict Types & Responses

**Financial conflicts:**
- Examples: Disputed expenses, unpaid contributions, unfair cost distribution
- Default resolution: Review financial records, check against this agreement, adjust if needed

**Labor conflicts:**
- Examples: One family not contributing, unfair task distribution, quality of work
- Default resolution: Review labor logs, discuss expectations, redistribute tasks, or financial compensation

**Harvest conflicts:**
- Examples: Unequal shares, someone taking more than agreed, timing of harvest
- Default resolution: Measure/track quantities, refer to Section 6 agreements, adjust distribution method if needed

**Interpersonal conflicts:**
- Examples: Communication style, personality clashes, boundary violations
- Default resolution: Step 1-2 mediation, focus on behavior not character

**Value/vision conflicts:**
- Examples: Chemical vs. organic, selling vs. gifting, aesthetics, animal welfare
- Default resolution: Refer to Section 1 (Core Principles), seek compromise that honors all perspectives
