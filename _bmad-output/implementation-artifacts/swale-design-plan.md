## 2. SWALE DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### What are Swales?
- Level ditches dug along contour lines (perpendicular to water flow)
- Capture and infiltrate rainwater runoff
- Prevent soil erosion
- Recharge groundwater
- Create microclimates for tree establishment

### Design Parameters for Your Site

#### Swale Dimensions (Recommended)
- **Width**: 3-4 feet (0.9-1.2 m) at top
- **Depth**: 1.5-2 feet (0.45-0.6 m)
- **Length**: ~830 feet (253 m) - nearly full E-W length
- **Spacing**: 40-60 feet (12-18 m) apart (depends on slope angle)
- **Berm height**: 1-1.5 feet (0.3-0.45 m) on downslope side

#### Recommended Number of Swales
Given your 209-foot width (N-S) and recommended spacing:
- **4-5 swales** across the property
- Spacing: approximately 50 feet apart

## 4. COMMUNITY BENEFITS & RESPONSIBILITIES

### How Swales Benefit the 4-Family Cluster

#### Direct Benefits to Housing Cluster:
1. **Flood Protection**: Swales prevent runoff from flowing back toward houses during heavy monsoon
2. **Greywater Management**: Zone 1 greywater can flow naturally toward Swale 1 (gravity-fed irrigation)
3. **Microclimate Cooling**: Trees along swales create cooler air that drifts north toward housing
4. **Food Production Access**: All families equidistant from swale tree lines (200-300 meters round trip)
5. **Children's Education**: Swales become outdoor classroom for observing water cycles, tree growth
6. **Community Gathering**: Swale berms = comfortable seating areas for evening socializing

#### Shared Responsibilities (Community Agreement Needed):

**Swale 1 Maintenance (Closest to Housing):**
- **Primary responsibility**: All 4 families (most benefit)
- **Tasks**: 
  - Quarterly: Check for erosion, debris removal
  - Annually: Prune trees for chop-and-drop mulch (distribute to Zone 1 gardens)
  - Post-monsoon: Clear spillways, check infiltration
- **Labor**: Rotate monthly among families (4-6 hours/quarter)

- **Primary responsibility**: Families 1 & 2 (nearest location)
- **Tasks**: 
  - Tree harvesting (fruit belongs to maintainers)
  - Inlet channel to pond maintenance
  - Spillway management

- **Primary responsibility**: Families 3 & 4 (nearest to SE pond)

  - Main pond inlet management (critical!)

### Phase 2: Earthworks (Weeks 3-5)

#### Equipment Needed
- Small excavator or tractor with box blade
- Dump truck (if offsite spoil disposal needed)
- Compaction equipment for berms

#### Construction Sequence
1. **Start from upslope (north)** and work downward
2. Excavate first swale
3. Form berm on downslope side with excavated soil
4. Compact berm to prevent erosion
5. Repeat for remaining swales
6. Create spillways every 150-200 feet for overflow

#### Quality Checks
- Verify swales are level (no ponding at ends)
- Check berm stability
- Ensure smooth transitions at ends
- Test water flow with hose during construction

### Phase 3: Soil Amendment (Week 6)

- [ ] Test soil in swales and berms
- [ ] Add compost/organic matter to planting areas
- [ ] Create biochar if available
- [ ] Prepare planting holes on berms (2x2x2 feet)
- [ ] Mix soil amendments in planting holes

### Phase 4: Tree Planting (Weeks 7-9)

#### Timing
- **Best time**: Beginning of monsoon season
- **Alternative**: Pre-monsoon with irrigation setup

#### Planting Protocol
1. Start with nitrogen fixers (Gliricidia, Subabul)
2. Plant on berms 15-20 feet apart
3. Stake tall saplings
4. Heavy mulch around each tree (4-inch layer)
5. Create small water basin around each tree
6. Water immediately after planting

#### Recommended Sizes
- Nitrogen fixers: 2-3 feet tall saplings or cuttings
- Fruit trees: 1-2 year old grafted saplings
- Fast-growers: Direct seeding possible

### Phase 5: Establishment (Months 3-12)

#### First Monsoon Season
- [ ] Monitor water flow and infiltration
- [ ] Repair any erosion damage
- [ ] Replant any failed trees (expect 5-10% mortality)
- [ ] Mulch replenishment

#### Ongoing Maintenance
- [ ] Weed management in swales (first 2 years)
- [ ] Pruning nitrogen fixers for mulch (chop & drop)
- [ ] Interplanting annuals between trees
- [ ] Monitor tree growth and health

## 7. POND INTEGRATION FOR WATER STORAGE & AQUACULTURE

### Design Rationale

#### Sealing Options

**Option A: Clay Lining (Most Natural)**
- 6-8 inch compacted clay layer
- Best for livestock integration
- Natural filtration
- Cost: â‚¹30,000-50,000

**Option B: Bentonite (If soil is sandy)**
- 2-3 inch bentonite layer mixed with soil
- Good water retention
- Cost: â‚¹50,000-80,000

**Option C: Liner (Most reliable)**
- HDPE or EPDM liner
- Longest lasting (20-30 years)
- Easiest for shaped edges
- Cost: â‚¹80,000-1,50,000
- Cover with soil layer for UV protection

**Option D: Natural Seal (Lowest cost)**
- Compact subsoil thoroughly
- Add organic matter (manure, straw)
- Allow to puddle naturally with water cycles
- May take 6-12 months to fully seal
- Cost: Minimal (labor only)
