## â“ FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### Design Questions

**Q: Can we change from Cluster Village to Linear housing?**
A: Yes, but you'll lose some efficiency (longer utility runs, less community interaction). Option 3 (Linear) is the second-best choice if cluster doesn't work for your families.

**Q: What if we want bigger/smaller houses?**
A: Houses can be 600-1,200 sq ft each. Adjust Zone 0 depth accordingly (may need 45-50 ft instead of 40 ft N-S).

**Q: Can we add more animals later?**
A: Yes! Design allows expansion. Could add goats, rabbits, quail if desired.

**Q: What if our soil test shows problems?**
A: Alkaline soil is expected in Haryana. Add organic matter (compost), sulfur, and grow appropriate plants. Permaculture methods improve soil over time.

### Financial Questions

**Q: What if we can't afford the full Year 1 investment?**
A: Phase the project:
- Year 1 Priority: Windbreaks, Zone 1 gardens only (â‚¹60,000-95,000 per family)
- Year 2: Swales, Zone 2 animals
- Year 3: Zones 3-4, pond

**Q: Can we make money from this farm?**
A: Yes! Surplus vegetables, eggs, and fruit can be sold. Timber in Years 10-30 is significant income. But primary goal is self-sufficiency, not profit.

**Q: Are these production estimates realistic?**
A: These are CONSERVATIVE estimates based on permaculture farm data. With good management, you can exceed them. Poor management will produce less.

### Community Questions

**Q: What if families don't get along?**
A: This is THE biggest risk. Spend time together BEFORE committing. Do a trial period (camp on land for weeks?). Create clear agreements. But if there's no compatibility, this won't work no matter how good the design.

**Q: What if one family leaves?**
A: Exit strategy in agreements (see above). Find replacement family with group approval. Worst case: Remaining families buy out leaver, or all sell and split proceeds.

**Q: Do all 4 families need to move in at once?**
A: No! Can build 2 houses in Year 1, 2 more in Year 2. But all families should participate in planning and early work even if not living on-site yet.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Will 12-14 swales really work better than 3?**
A: Yes! Shorter swales are easier to level and construct. More swales = better water distribution and system redundancy. This is a proven technique.

**Q: What about snakes in the pond?**
A: Ponds attract beneficial wildlife (frogs, dragonflies) which eat pests. Non-venomous water snakes are generally harmless. Keep area clear around pond.

**Q: Can we grow rice?**
A: Not recommended in this design (requires flooding). Stick to dryland grains (wheat, millet, sorghum) which suit permaculture and local climate better.

**Q: What about summer heat (40Â°C+)?**
A: Passive solar house design, shade cloth over nursery, mulching, adequate water. Windbreaks and trees will cool property over time. First summers will be tough, then it improves!
