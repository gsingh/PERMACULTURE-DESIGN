## 2. SWALE DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### What are Swales?
- **Function:** Level ditches dug along contour lines (perpendicular to water flow)
- **Purpose:** Capture and infiltrate rainwater runoff, recharge groundwater
- **Benefits:** Prevent erosion, create tree microclimates, passive irrigation
- **Direction:** Run East-West (perpendicular to N-S slope)

### Design Parameters for Your Site

#### Standard Swale Dimensions
- **Width (top):** 3-4 feet (0.9-1.2 m)
- **Depth:** 1.5-2 feet (0.45-0.6 m)
- **Length:** ~209 feet (64 m) - full E-W width
- **Berm height:** 1-1.5 feet (0.3-0.45 m) on downslope side
- **Side slopes:** 2:1 or 3:1 (gentle for stability)

#### Water Holding Capacity (per swale)
- **Cross-section area:** ~6-8 sq ft
- **Volume per swale:** 6-8 sq ft Ã— 209 ft = 1,254-1,672 cu ft
- **Gallons per swale:** ~9,400-12,500 gallons
- **5 swales total:** ~47,000-62,500 gallons storage

#### Monsoon Design Criteria
- **Peak rainfall:** 100-150mm in 24 hours (monsoon storms)
- **Runoff coefficient:** 0.4-0.6 (partial vegetation coverage)
- **Catchment area per swale:** ~40,000-80,000 sq ft (varies by position)
- **Required capacity:** Each swale designed for 2-3 day storm

## 6. POND DESIGN & INTEGRATION

- Shape: Circular
- Diameter: 30-35 ft

- **Shade:** Plant trees on north/west sides (afternoon shade)

**Guest Room Integration:**
- **Location:** 760 ft (20 ft from pond edge)
